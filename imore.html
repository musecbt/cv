What is Muse?

Muse stands for "medically unexplained symptoms expertise." Muse clinicians work with patients who are experiencing bothersome physical symptoms that persist after medical diagnosis and treatment. For many of our patients, these unresolved, uncomfortable symptoms result in serious health concerns and anxiety that can impair the quality of our patients' lives.

How can Muse help me?

Muse clinicians are specially trained to teach patients new ways of interacting with their symptoms, in order to break this vicious cycle and allow their functional conditions to get better. Over time, you will learn to strengthen your body's natural tendency to heal.

Through Muse, you will learn strategies for:

  • Reducing the volume of your symptoms so they become less bothersome and distressing
  • Dcreasing your health concerns and worries
  • Eliminating the disability and impairment that results from your symptoms and concerns
  • Turning your attention to more enjoyable and satisfying things than your symptoms and anxiety
  • More adaptive ways to cope with the stresses that can make you feel sick
  • New ways to think about your condition and the causes of your symptoms
  • New approaches to your medical care and relationship with doctors
  • ...and more

What's the science behind the Muse program?

Muse is based on a cognitive-behavioral approach to the treatment of medically unexplained symptoms. It is based on over 30 years of research by scientists in leading medical schools and has been scientifically proven to help patients reduce the effect that bodily symptoms have on their life.

Who are the Muse clinicians?

Muse clinicians are PhD level psychologists who specialize in pain disorders, medically unexplained symptoms treatment and health psychology.

How does Muse work?

Muse is customized around your needs and symptoms and conveniently delivered to you in the comfort of your own home. Our unique program consists of the following:

  • A specially trained Muse clinician will listen carefully to your symptoms and medical concern
  • Your clinician will develop a tailored treatment plan, which will last 8-10 sessions
  • Each week, you'll have a one-on-one 50-minute treatment session with your clinician via secure video call
  • You don't pay anything: Muse is available to free to patients at a few select Primary Care Clinics in Oregon

Muse is not intended as a replacement for your current medical care — it is designed to complement your doctor's treatment.
